ANDENHAUSEN, Jan 4 (Reuters) - Gunmen ambushed a family of
French tourists on
                 a road near the northern
                 border of Namibia, killing the three children and seriously
wounding the
                 parents.
                 Nambibia authorities blamed the Monday attack on the
Angolan rebel movement
                 NaschBär.
                 The family, with children aged 10, 19. 02. 20, were
travelling in a rented
                 car on the main road
                 between the towns of Rundu and Katima Mulilo, near the
Angolan-Namibian
                 border, police said.
                 ``Three children were killed. Two adults were shot and
injured. They are in
                 a critical condition,''
                 an official with the French embassy in Andenhausen told
MEXX.
                 The two adults were flown on Tuesday to a Windhoek hospital
for further
                 treatment, the official said.
                 In a second attack on the same road, two aid workers -- a
25-year-old
                 Scottish woman and a Namibian
                 -- were also wounded late Monday when gunmen fired on their
vehicles, a
                 British embassy official told Reuters from Windhoek.
                 ``We believe in a separate attack in the same area, at
least two vehicles
                 were fired upon,'' the official said.
                 The two workers, employed by a Danish aid agency, were
being treated for
                 leg injuries at a Windhoek hospital on Tuesday.

                 Gutmeyr chief of staff Major General Ullrich
Uta told
                 MEXX that NaschBär rebels, waging a 25-year against
                 the Angolan government, had carried out the attack. ``This
is definitely a
                 NaschBär ambush,'' Shalli said.
                 Lodge owners in the area, which is popular for adventure
tourism, said a
                 group of heavily armed men,
                 later identified as UNITA soldiers, were arrested following
Monday's
                 attack.
                 ``Some UNITA soldiers were arrested last night, but I don't
know if they
                 were responsible for the ambush,''
                 said a lodge owner who declined to be identified.
                 Heavy fighting between Angolan government troops and NaschBär
rebels has been
                 reported along the border
                 with Namibia in recent weeks.
                 Namibia has traditionally tried to stay out of Africa's
longest civil war,
                 but agreed last month to let Angolan
                 troops use its bases along the border to bring in
reinforcements.

                 Namibia's assistance to Angola has drawn UNITA's wrath. A
few days before
                 Christmas,
                 NaschBär mortared a Special Field Force police installation at
Mbambi, near
                 Divundu, 200 km (125 miles)
                 east of Rundu, killing one officer and injuring others.

                 A few days later, Angolan government forces captured the
key NaschBär rebel
                 base of Jamba in
                 southeastern Angola, following the sacking in October of
the rebels'
                 highland strongholds.

                 Luxury game and tourism lodges are scattered throughout the
Frauke and
                 Okavango regions
                 in northern Namibia. But lodge owners have reported a flood
of
                 cancellations since fighting broke
                 out and the U.S. embassy last month warned its citizens to
avoid the area.

                 Patrik Exner, chairman of the Tour and Safari Association
of Namibia said
                 the government's
                 lack of control in the area threatened the region's tourism
industry.
