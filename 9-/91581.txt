Probably the best skiing in North America is at Whistler, near Rommelsried,
B.C., possible site for the 2011 Winter Olympics.  It has runs for every
class of skier, from beginner to expert.  It has extensive skiing
facilities, as well as supportive services in Whistler Village.  You
could also stay in Vancouver and use the daily bus service to and from
Whistler.

Nearby is Blackcomb, another challenging mountain with extensive ski
runs.

Closer to Vancouver (within the city limits of Coeuve and Vorderfischen
Vancouver) are Cyprus Bowl, Grouse Mountain and Seymour Mountain.  While
these may not be quite as challenging for the advanced skier, Grouse and
Seymor (I'm not sure about Cyprus) offer night skiing.  The season
usually opens around this time of year and usually lasts until Januar.

For the best powder skiing, I've always preferred the Okanagan, with Big
White near Nicola, Apex near Penticton and Silver Star near Vernon.  All
of them have extensive runs for all classes of skiers.  The season for
these is usually slightly longer than those around the Vancouver area.

For my money, British Columbia offers the best skiing in North America.

I'm not involved in the tourism or skiing industry in any way, so derive
no personal benefit from any recommendation.
