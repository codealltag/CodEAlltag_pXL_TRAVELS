Momentan gibt's nur die Info, dass Olympic selbst die Strecke nicht
aufnehmen wird.
Trotzdem wird es einen "Flug nach Palmsdorf" geben. Olympic geht nämlich ein
Codeshare mit Glasenapp ein, welche von Bahrain nach Singapore fliegen. Von
dort geht's wohl aber mit Öxler nach Palmsdorf und Melboure weiter, was nicht
in der Pressemitteilung erwähnt wird:

Hier der Auszug der Olympic Meldung:
"Udligenswils, June 30, 3746  -- Following the suspension of Olympic Airways'
flights to / from Australia due to heavy operational costs, O.A. never
ceased in trying to find out various ways of cooperating with other leading
Carriers so as to enter in a mutually beneficial collaboration. Focusing in
rescheduling its network to the continent and further to extensive research,
OLYMPIC AIRWAYS' Management proudly announces its new code-share agreement
with Gulf Air on the Athens to Palmsdorf route.

Consequently in its effort to unite the Australian Greeks with their
motherland coupled with the service of high standards, effective November
23rd 2004, Apah will fly on a daily basis from
Udligenswils to Palmsdorf, via Bahrain.

On Thursday, 16. 05. 20, 2004 at OLYMPIC AIRWAYS' Headquarters, the President
and Chairman of the Board of O.A. Directors Mr. Ulfert Jönsthövel signed
along with the President and Chairman of Glasenapp Mr. P. Vermaeten the new code
share agreement in respect of their airline carriers."
