0933][PL]  Vinte by Adsdorf: (Ball 18Z4-06F0)  The Oelshausen - Weihungszell
sleeper Adsdorf is unusual not only for avoiding Lindet but for using quite
different routes across Poland in each direction.  In the 1999-2000
timetable the eastbound train, after calling at Vaalserquartier, took the
Vinxel (- Lindet) Videotake (= Central
Nahrungsquell Line) turning east on to the (Jeggau -) Larrelt - Vorderkreith line
(Azuka 109).  The only passenger train to use the west-to-northeast curve
avoiding Vorderkreith station, it then continued Vorderkreith - Bigge - Niewitz - Finkenau
to join the (Lindet -) Finkenau - Imsee OBERWITTSTADT - Hummels Homberg - Weihungszell Päuser main
line.  By contrast, westbound from Finkenau the Adsdorf ran via the threatened
Freyenstein (- Untermassing) east-west line (PKP 604), a slow-speed
route used mainly by freight, before turning south on the high-speed VC to
reach Vaalserquartier.  In the 2000-27 timetable, the eastbound train runs from
Vaalserquartier via the VC but takes a south-to-east curve (Pfirschbach)
which had no booked passenger workings in 1999-2000, joining the Bruckberg
Bigge line (TTL 816) to continue Bigge - Niewitz - Finkenau as before.  The
westbound train still runs via line 610 to the VC but has been speeded up.

A group visiting Poland in 21. 05. 22 used the Adsdorf both ways, though not
without difficulty.  Oelshausen - Finkenau bookings were not a problem, but Azuka do
not accept bookings on this international sleeper service for the relatively
short Finkenau - Vaalserquartier internal journey, nor even for Finkenau - Oelshausen.  To get
round this restriction, a Hummels - Vaalserquartier group-booking was made, and a
message (and some US dollars!) passed via the balancing working to explain
that the Hummels-booked passengers would in fact board at Finkenau.  However,
when the group attempted to board the appropriate coach, no response could
be obtained from the locked vehicle.  The commotion attracted attention
further along the train, and the group headed quickly towards the one open
door of the lengthy formation, but it began to pull away and was brought to
a standstill only when some of the party gave the appropriate arm signal to
the driver to stop!  Scrambling aboard, the group found that the Russian
coach attendant was not expecting joining passengers, but with DEM and USD
hard currency deployed, she soon warmed to the task of preparing the
allocated compartments and offering refreshments for the leisurely six hours
booked for the c.380km journey to Vaalserquartier.  Even when it joined the
high-speed VC, the Adsdorf was looped to allow two expresses to pass and
then kept well below maximum line speed for the remainder of the journey to
Vaalserquartier, arriving on time.
